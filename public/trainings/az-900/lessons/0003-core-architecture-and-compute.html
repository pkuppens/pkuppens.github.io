Core Architecture, Compute & Networking

Domain 2 is the heaviest, and this is its foundation: how Azure organizes resources, and how you run compute and connect it. Every term here has a direct AWS/GCP analog — the work is remapping vocabulary, not learning new concepts.

The organizational hierarchy

Azure scopeClosest AWS/GCP equivalentPurpose
Management groupAWS Organizations OUGoverns multiple subscriptions at once
SubscriptionAWS account / GCP project (billing)Billing and scale boundary
Resource group(no direct 1:1 equivalent)Lifecycle/RBAC container for related resources
ResourceIndividual service instanceA specific VM, storage account, etc.
Exam cue A tenant (your Entra ID directory) sits above subscriptions and isn't part of the resource hierarchy itself — one tenant can contain many subscriptions. RBAC and Policy assigned at a higher scope are inherited downward.

Regions, zones, and pairs

ConstructProtects against
Availability zone (same term as AWS AZ)Loss of one datacenter within a region — synchronous replication
Region pairLoss of an entire region — async DR, coordinated platform updates
Sovereign region (e.g. Azure Government)Not physical failure — legal/compliance isolation from global Azure
